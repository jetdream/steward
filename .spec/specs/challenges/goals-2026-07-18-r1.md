FINDINGS:

- [high] The per-goal complete/partial split is not derivable from the policy's own
  stated definition, and leaves the INC-2 false-achievability defect standing in G-1
  and G-4 — the compass lens's "coherence" failure. The intent defines `complete` as
  "no capability the metric plausibly requires is missing from the serving set." By
  that rule G-5 was correctly downgraded (its set can't produce a live post). But the
  same rule condemns G-1 and G-4, which were kept `complete`:
  - **G-1** (orgs pay & stay): serving set is `BIL, NWS, ONB-1..5, OPS, PUB` — the
    publish pipe (PUB) and QA (OPS) but **neither content generation (GEN) nor
    approval (APR)**. A set that cannot produce a single post to publish cannot be a
    complete decomposition of "orgs pay for the product," yet the prose asserts "no
    capability gap in the commercial funnel itself." G-5's own downgrade sentence —
    "as pinned this goal cannot produce its own metric's subject" — is *more* true of
    G-1.
  - **G-4** (4-week streak): pins `PUB` but **not APR/AUT (approval)** — an interior
    step of the live-post chain is absent. Its reasoning literally concedes this ("a
    post only counts toward a streak once it goes LIVE, which requires… approve").
  The only device separating these from G-5 is "cross-goal reliance (explicit, not a
  gap)," which rests on no stated criterion — indistinguishable, as written, from
  G-5's situation. "Names the reliance in prose" is not a real distinction from "has
  the gap"; it is the very prose-substitution INC-2 rejected. Consequence is live:
  once specs land the roll-up flips G-1/G-4 to `achieved` on a serving set that
  excludes generation/approval — precisely what INC-2 was opened to kill. The pass
  resolves INC-2 for one of five goals and re-instantiates it in two others.
  Fix (either): (a) state the operative rule in the register intent — an
  end-to-end/latency goal must pin every capability on its critical path, an
  aspect/failure-mode goal pins its aspect's capabilities and names assumed-working
  sub-chains — and re-derive each status from it; or (b) apply the Q-12 move
  (add the `serves` edges, v-bump cascade) so the roll-up gates on the real critical
  path, not prose.

- [low] Altitude leak: coverage reasoning restates requirement-layer specifics and
  priorities the goals layer should reference, not carry — e.g. `BIL-1 ($199, card at
  signup, no trial)`, `PUB-1 (…±5 min…)`, `NWS-6 (P1)`, `PUB-4 … P2`. Unversioned,
  will drift from the requirement register. Fix: keep the ID + its causal role, drop
  the parameter/priority parentheticals.
